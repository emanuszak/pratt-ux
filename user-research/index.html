community volunteering platform

Project Goals

Design a new product for DNAinfo to help users connect with their neighborhoods through local volunteer opportunities.

Problem Definition

Interest to volunteer and do social good in the community tends to surge around events–holidays, this year's presidential election, natural disasters, etc.

Volunteers, however, rely on word-of-mouth and broad internet searches to find organizations that fit their causes, showing that there is no clear-cut destination for volunteer opportunities. This means users spend significant time digging through search results and vetting organizations instead of participating in volunteer activities.

http://www.dailykos.com/story/2016/11/17/1600883/-Progressive-organizations-see-a-surge-in-donations-and-volunteers-after-Trump-s-election

Problem Validation

Target Audience

College-educated professionals in South Brooklyn (Downtown Brooklyn, Carroll Gardens, Cobble Hill, Gowanus, Park Slope, Red Hook), age 20-40, recent or renewed interested in social good but does not identify as an activist.

Research

Interviews

Interview Insights

Behaviors

  • users search by highly individual interests
  • activities requiring research are done on desktop, not mobile
  • mainly use mobile for browsing and simple tasks
  • increased motivation when something affects the community

Channels

  • high Facebook usage
  • word-of-mouth referrals
  • internet searches
  • marketing from organizations

Challenges

  • tendency to be passive over active about volunteer opportunities
  • motivation is fleeting, often time-sensitive
  • inconsistent communication from organization

Pain Points

  • forms on mobile are cumbersome
  • large time investment researching for opportunities

Product Opportunities

Based on the conducted research, there are opportunities to reduce the amount of friction experienced for users signing up for events on mobile devices, and to help surface volunteer activities for users so they can spend less time searching on their own.

Feature Framework

The proposed initial feature will provide a streamlined list of search results intended for quick browsing, and one-click signup/registration using social network authentication (Facebook and Google). After registering the user has the option to complete a profile to receive personalized recommendations for future volunteering.

Secondary features could include calendar integration and feedback on the organization and volunteer experience.

User Archetypes

The Active Volunteer

designed by Freepik from Flaticon

Traits

  • Dedicated to volunteering on a consistent basis, perhaps once or twice a month
  • Proactive in seeking volunteer activities in their spare time
  • Motivation is closely tied to causes they care about
  • Open to doing a range of volunteer activities, as long as the organization fits their personal beliefs

Needs

  • Sift through opportunities quickly
  • Easily identify relevant listings
  • More uniform way of reaching out/applying for volunteer opportunities
  • Better insight into the organization

The Passive Participant

designed by Freepik from Flaticon

Traits

  • May volunteer once or twice a year at most
  • Open to learning about opportunities from friends or Facebook but does not seek out volunteering on their own
  • Convenience or reward as a primary motivator
  • Typically driven by a catalyst to volunteer, such as an event
  • Susceptible to peer-pressure or social validation

Needs

  • Volunteer opportunity must be presented to or surfaced for them
  • Quick and easy signup process
  • Activities must fit with their schedule

Design Principles

  • Be considerate of users' time
  • Be accessible
  • Bite-sized
  • Conversational but not at the sake of being informative
  • Nothing exists in a vacuum

User Flow

  1. Open the site on landing page
  2. Scrolls to browse interest areas and selects one
  3. Scrolls through results, clicks a listing
  4. Wants to sign up for opportunity
  5. Registers with Google/FB OAuth
  6. Confirms signup, opting in to create profile
  7. Continues on to complete user profile
